In the Philippines, it’s ‘so hot you can’t
breathe’ as heat index touches 47 degrees celcius
news health
Laughing Cow

12 Dec, 2019 11:23 am

At least thirty cities and municipalities have hit "danger" levels for the heat index, which is a measurement of how hot it feels while taking humidity into consideration.

Additionally, there was a 50% probability that the heat will get worse over the next few days, according to the head climatologist for the Philippine state weather forecaster.

The Philippines was sweltering forcing several schools to cancel in-person courses and prompting advisories to citizens to spend as little time outside as possible.

The nation of the archipelago usually has the hottest and driest months in March, April, and May, but this year's El Nino weather phenomena has made things worse.

Source: Inquirer.net
